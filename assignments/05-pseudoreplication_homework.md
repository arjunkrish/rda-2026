**Questions:**

1. You are reviewing a mouse study where pups from the same litter are treated as independent replicates. The paper reports n=30 (pups) but there are only 5 litters. What specific statistical problem does this create, and what would you recommend the authors do instead?

2. In the Blainey et al. framework, biological variance is usually much larger than technical variance. If that's true, why would anyone ever invest in technical replicates? Under what experimental circumstances does it actually make sense to prioritize technical over biological replication?

3. The Gilad & Mizrahi-Man reanalysis showed that correcting for batch in the mouse ENCODE data reversed the main biological conclusion. But one reviewer pointed out that if batch and species are nearly perfectly confounded, applying ComBat effectively removes the species signal too — making it impossible to recover the true answer from this dataset. Does that mean Gilad's correction was wrong? What does this situation reveal about the limits of post-hoc batch correction vs. study design?

4. Leek et al. argue that normalization does not remove batch effects and can even exacerbate them. Why? What is it about the nature of batch effects that makes standard normalization insufficient?
